Answer Key & Marking Guide

Criterion A

  • 1. Continuous (Numerical, ratio scale)
  • 2. 15 (Order: 12, 12, 15, 15, 15, 18, 22)
  • 3. x + 8 (Range = Max - Min = (2x+3) - (x-5))
  • 4. 9 (Total must be 50; 8+12+7+14 = 41)
  • 5. 10 ≤ x < 20 (Highest frequency of 12)

Criterion B

  • 6. Dataset A (All values equal, no deviation)
  • 7. Doubles (Linear transformation: new Mean = old Mean * 2)
  • 8. ~40 (Approx Q3=65, Q1=25)
  • 9. 50% (Definition of Median)
  • 10. Positive (Tail extends to the right/higher end)

Criterion C

  • 11. Time-series data (Line graphs are for trends; Pie is for parts of a whole)
  • 12. Q3 + 1.5 * IQR (Upper boundary for outliers)
  • 13. 75% (Q3 represents the 75th percentile)
  • 14. Gaps (Bar charts have gaps; Histograms for continuous data don't)
  • 15. Sampling Bias (Sample taken from a gym is not representative of the general population)

Criterion D

  • 16. 68% (Within ±1 standard deviation)
  • 17. < 130g (Values below -2 sigma)
  • 18. Variance (Extreme heavy apples could break it even if average is fine)
  • 19. Mean increases significantly (Median stays stable; Mean is non-resistant)
  • 20. Weight > 160g (Top 16% starts at +1 sigma)

AoPS Challenge Solutions

  • 21. 162 (Smallest sum occurs when middle elements are shared and maximized)
  • 22. m² (Variance is multiplied by the square of the factor; adding k doesn't change spread)
  • 23. 100 (Max product when a = b = 10)
  • 24. Correlation reversal (Weighted averages across subgroups mask the overall trend)
  • 25. Set X (σ=2) (Lower spread means higher probability density around the center)
