📐 Basic Trigonometric Ratios

Understanding sine, cosine, and tangent in right-angled triangles (Haese Chapter 12)

The Right-Angled Triangle

In a right-angled triangle, the side opposite the right angle is called the hypotenuse (the longest side). The other two sides are called adjacent (next to the angle) and opposite (opposite the angle).

Interactive: Explore Triangle Sides

Drag the slider to change the angle and see how sides change

SOHCAHTOA

These three ratios relate the angles of a right triangle to the lengths of its sides:

\(\sin(\theta) = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}\)
\(\cos(\theta) = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}\)
\(\tan(\theta) = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}}\)
Memory Tip: SOH-CAH-TOA = Sine=Opp/Hyp, Cosine=Adj/Hyp, Tangent=Opp/Adj
Ratio30°45°60°
\(\sin\)\(\frac{1}{2}\)\(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\)\(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\)
\(\cos\)\(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\)\(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\)\(\frac{1}{2}\)
\(\tan\)\(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}\)\(1\)\(\sqrt{3}\)

📝 Example 1: Finding a Side Length

Problem: In a right triangle, the hypotenuse is 10 cm and the angle is 30°. Find the opposite side.

Solution: Use \(\sin(\theta) = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}\)

\(\sin(30°) = \frac{x}{10}\)

\(x = 10 \times \sin(30°) = 10 \times 0.5 = 5\) cm

🌍 Real-World: Measuring Tree Height

Scenario: A surveyor stands 20 meters from a tree. Using a clinometer, they measure the angle of elevation to the top of the tree as 52°. How tall is the tree?

Solution: \(\tan(52°) = \frac{\text{height}}{20}\)

\(\text{height} = 20 \times \tan(52°) \approx 20 \times 1.2799 \approx 25.6\) meters

👁️ Angles of Elevation & Depression

Solving problems involving looking up or down (Haese Chapter 12)

Angle of Elevation

The angle measured upwards from the horizontal to look at an object above.

Angle of Depression

The angle measured downwards from the horizontal to look at an object below.

Key Insight: The angle of depression from point A to point B equals the angle of elevation from point B to point A (alternate angles).

📝 Example 2: Building Height

Problem: From the top of a 50m tall building, the angle of depression to a car on the ground is 28°. How far is the car from the base of the building?

Solution: The angle of depression = angle of elevation from car = 28°

\(\tan(28°) = \frac{50}{x}\) where x is the distance from building base

\(x = \frac{50}{\tan(28°)} \approx \frac{50}{0.5317} \approx 94.0\) meters

🌍 Real-World: Aviation Navigation

Scenario: An airplane flying at 10,000 ft altitude spots an airport at an angle of depression of 15°. The pilot wants to know the horizontal distance to start descent.

Solution:

\(\tan(15°) = \frac{10000}{d}\)

\(d = \frac{10000}{\tan(15°)} \approx \frac{10000}{0.2679} \approx 37,320\) ft ≈ 7.1 miles

Pilots use this calculation to determine when to begin their descent approach.

🧭 Bearings

Navigation using three-figure bearings (Haese Chapter 12)

What are Bearings?

A bearing is a direction measured as a three-figure angle clockwise from north.

  • Always 3 digits (045°, not 45°)
  • Measured clockwise from North
  • Range: 000° to 359°
  • Due North = 000°, East = 090°, South = 180°, West = 270°

Interactive Compass

Solving Bearing Problems

Use trigonometry to find distances and positions when given bearings.

📝 Example 3: Ship Navigation

Problem: A ship sails 15 km on a bearing of 060°, then 10 km on a bearing of 150°. How far is it from the starting point?

Solution: Draw the path, find the angle between paths (150° - 60° = 90°), then use Pythagoras or Cosine Rule.

\(d = \sqrt{15^2 + 10^2} = \sqrt{225 + 100} = \sqrt{325} \approx 18.03\) km

🌍 Real-World: Maritime Navigation

Scenario: A ship leaves port on bearing 035°, sails 120 nautical miles. Then changes course to bearing 125°, sails 80 nautical miles. Find final position relative to port.

This type of calculation is used by ships and aircraft for GPS-free navigation.

📏 The Sine Rule

For non-right angled triangles (Haese Chapter 25)

Sine Rule Formula

Used when you know either:

  • Two angles and one side (AAS or ASA)
  • Two sides and an opposite angle (SSA)
\(\frac{a}{\sin(A)} = \frac{b}{\sin(B)} = \frac{c}{\sin(C)}\)

or equivalently:

\(\frac{\sin(A)}{a} = \frac{\sin(B)}{b} = \frac{\sin(C)}{c}\)

Interactive: Sine Rule Triangle

Angles change automatically keep sum = 180°

Worked Examples

📝 Example 4: Finding a Side

Given: Triangle ABC with A = 50°, B = 70°, a = 12 cm. Find side b.

Solution: \(\frac{a}{\sin(A)} = \frac{b}{\sin(B)}\)

\(\frac{12}{\sin(50°)} = \frac{b}{\sin(70°)}\)

\(b = \frac{12 \times \sin(70°)}{\sin(50°)} \approx \frac{12 \times 0.9397}{0.7660} \approx 14.72\) cm

📝 Example 5: Finding an Angle

Given: Triangle with a = 8 cm, b = 10 cm, A = 35°. Find angle B.

Solution: \(\frac{\sin(A)}{a} = \frac{\sin(B)}{b}\)

\(\sin(B) = \frac{b \times \sin(A)}{a} = \frac{10 \times \sin(35°)}{8} \approx \frac{10 \times 0.5736}{8} \approx 0.717\)

\(B = \sin^{-1}(0.717) \approx 45.8°\) or \(180° - 45.8° = 134.2°\) (ambiguous case!)

Ambiguous Case (SSA): When given two sides and a non-included angle, there may be 0, 1, or 2 possible triangles. Always check if both solutions are valid.

🌍 Real-World: Triangulation in Surveying

Scenario: Surveyors need to find the distance across a river without crossing it. They measure a baseline AB = 200m, then measure angles: from A, angle to point C is 65°; from B, angle to C is 55°. Find distance AC.

Solution: Angle C = 180° - 65° - 55° = 60°

Using Sine Rule: \(\frac{AC}{\sin(55°)} = \frac{200}{\sin(60°)}\)

\(AC = \frac{200 \times \sin(55°)}{\sin(60°)} \approx \frac{200 \times 0.8192}{0.8660} \approx 189.2\) meters

📐 The Cosine Rule

For non-right angled triangles when Sine Rule doesn't apply (Haese Chapter 25)

Cosine Rule Formula

Use when you know:

  • Three sides (SSS)
  • Two sides and the included angle (SAS)
\(a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc\cos(A)\)
\(b^2 = a^2 + c^2 - 2ac\cos(B)\)
\(c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab\cos(C)\)

To find an angle when all sides are known, rearrange:

\(\cos(A) = \frac{b^2 + c^2 - a^2}{2bc}\)

Interactive: Cosine Rule Triangle

Worked Examples

📝 Example 6: Finding a Side (SAS)

Given: Triangle with b = 8 cm, c = 12 cm, A = 45°. Find side a.

Solution: \(a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc\cos(A)\)

\(a^2 = 8^2 + 12^2 - 2(8)(12)\cos(45°)\)

\(a^2 = 64 + 144 - 192 \times 0.7071 = 208 - 135.77 = 72.23\)

\(a = \sqrt{72.23} \approx 8.50\) cm

📝 Example 7: Finding an Angle (SSS)

Given: Triangle with a = 7 cm, b = 8 cm, c = 9 cm. Find angle C.

Solution: \(\cos(C) = \frac{a^2 + b^2 - c^2}{2ab}\)

\(\cos(C) = \frac{49 + 64 - 81}{2(7)(8)} = \frac{32}{112} = 0.2857\)

\(C = \cos^{-1}(0.2857) \approx 73.4°\)

Pythagoras Check: When angle C = 90°, the Cosine Rule becomes \(c^2 = a^2 + b^2\) (Pythagoras' Theorem) since \(\cos(90°) = 0\).

🌍 Real-World: Bridge Design

Scenario: Engineers are designing a bridge support. Two structural beams of lengths 15m and 20m meet at an angle of 75°. What is the length of the connecting beam?

Solution: Using Cosine Rule with a = 15, b = 20, C = 75°

\(c^2 = 15^2 + 20^2 - 2(15)(20)\cos(75°)\)

\(c^2 = 225 + 400 - 600 \times 0.2588 = 625 - 155.28 = 469.72\)

\(c = \sqrt{469.72} \approx 21.67\) meters

📊 Area of a Triangle Using Sine

When you know two sides and the included angle (Haese Chapter 25)

Area Formula

Traditional area = \(\frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}\) requires knowing the height. The sine formula works when you know two sides and the included angle:

\(\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2}ab\sin(C)\)
\(\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2}bc\sin(A)\)
\(\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2}ac\sin(B)\)

Interactive: Area Visualization

Area: 0 square units

Worked Examples

📝 Example 8: Finding Area

Given: Triangle with sides 8 cm and 10 cm, included angle 35°.

Solution: \(\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2}(8)(10)\sin(35°)\)

\(\text{Area} = 40 \times 0.5736 = 22.94\) cm²

📝 Example 9: Maximum Area

Question: For a triangle with two fixed sides of length 10 and 15, what angle gives maximum area?

Solution: Area = \(\frac{1}{2}(10)(15)\sin(C) = 75\sin(C)\)

Maximum occurs when \(\sin(C) = 1\), so \(C = 90°\)

Maximum Area = 75 square units

🌍 Real-World: Land Surveying

Scenario: A triangular plot of land has two sides measuring 85m and 120m with an included angle of 68°. Calculate the area for property tax assessment.

Solution: \(\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2}(85)(120)\sin(68°)\)

\(\text{Area} = 5100 \times 0.9272 \approx 4728.7\) m² ≈ 0.47 hectares

📈 Trigonometric Graphs

Visualizing sine, cosine, and tangent functions

Sine and Cosine Curves

\(y = \sin(x)\)

Range: [-1, 1], Period: 360° or \(2\pi\)

  • Starts at (0, 0)
  • Maximum at 90°: y = 1
  • Zero at 180°
  • Minimum at 270°: y = -1
\(y = \cos(x)\)

Range: [-1, 1], Period: 360° or \(2\pi\)

  • Starts at (0, 1)
  • Zero at 90°
  • Minimum at 180°: y = -1
  • Maximum at 360°: y = 1

Interactive: Sine and Cosine Graphs

Tangent Curve

\(y = \tan(x) = \frac{\sin(x)}{\cos(x)}\)

Period: 180° or \(\pi\). Vertical asymptotes where \(\cos(x) = 0\).

Asymptotes: The tangent function has vertical asymptotes at x = 90°, 270°, etc. (where cos(x) = 0). The graph approaches infinity near these points.

🌍 Real-World Applications

How trigonometry is used in various fields

🏗️ Architecture

Roof Trusses: Engineers use trig to calculate rafter lengths and roof angles.

Example: For a roof with 30° pitch and 8m span, each rafter length = \(\frac{4}{\cos(30°)} \approx 4.62\) m

✈️ Aviation

Flight Path: Pilots calculate ground speed and drift angle using wind triangles.

Example: Aircraft heading 045° at 250 knots with 30 knot wind from 120°. Trig gives actual track and ground speed.

🌊 Physics - Waves

Sound & Light: All waves modeled using sine/cosine functions.

Formula: \(y = A\sin(2\pi ft + \phi)\) where A = amplitude, f = frequency, \(\phi\) = phase.

🔭 Astronomy

Parallax: Distance to stars calculated using angle measurements from opposite sides of Earth's orbit.

\(d = \frac{1 \text{ AU}}{\tan(p)}\) where p is parallax angle in arcseconds.

📱 GPS Technology

Triangulation: GPS uses signals from multiple satellites to calculate position using trigonometric principles.

Requires solving 3D triangles using extended trig formulas.

🎮 Computer Graphics

3D Rendering: Rotation matrices use sine and cosine to rotate objects in 3D space.

\(x' = x\cos(\theta) - y\sin(\theta)\) for 2D rotation.

🌍 Comprehensive Example: Solar Panel Optimization

Scenario: A solar panel in California (latitude 34°N) needs optimal tilt angle for maximum winter sun capture. The sun's angle at noon in winter is 32° above horizon.

Solution:

1. Panel should face south (northern hemisphere)

2. Optimal tilt = latitude ± seasonal adjustment = 34° + 15° = 49° from horizontal

3. If panel is 2m × 1.5m, effective area catching sun = \(2 \times 1.5 \times \sin(49°)\) = 3 × 0.7547 = 2.26 m²

This calculation helps maximize solar energy collection efficiency!
